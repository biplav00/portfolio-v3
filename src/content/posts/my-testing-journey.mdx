Six years ago, I started my QA journey as a junior tester. Today, I lead test automation initiatives. Here's what I've learned.

## The Journey

### 2018 - 2020: Junior QA Engineer

Started with manual testing, learned the fundamentals of bug tracking and test case management.

### 2020 - 2022: QA Engineer

First exposure to automation. Learned Selenium, wrote my first automated tests.

### 2022 - Present: Senior QA Engineer

Leading test strategy, mentoring junior testers, implementing CI/CD pipelines.

## Key Lessons Learned

### 1. Automation is a Mindset

> "Testing is not about finding bugs—it's about reducing risk."

Automation isn't about replacing manual testers; it's about freeing up time for exploratory testing and critical thinking.

### 2. Communication is Everything

The best QA engineers aren't the ones who know the most—they're the ones who can communicate effectively with developers, product managers, and stakeholders.

### 3. Never Stop Learning

Technology evolves fast. Stay updated with:

- New testing frameworks
- CI/CD best practices
- Security testing
- Performance testing

### 4. Build Relationships

Your network matters. Some of my best opportunities came from connections I made at meetups and online communities.

## Looking Ahead

The future of QA is exciting:

- **AI-assisted testing** - Smart test generation
- **Shift-left testing** - Testing earlier in the cycle
- **Quality engineering** - Building quality in, not testing it out

## Advice for New Testers

1. **Start with manual testing** - Understand the fundamentals
2. **Learn to code** - Even basic scripting helps
3. **Ask questions** - No question is too basic
4. **Share knowledge** - Teaching reinforces learning
5. **Be patient** - Mastery takes time

## Conclusion

My journey in QA has been challenging but rewarding. The key is to stay curious, keep learning, and never forget that behind every test is a user who deserves a quality experience.
